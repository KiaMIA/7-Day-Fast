Stages of Fasting

How your body transforms over 7 days

12 Hours
Glycogen Depletion
◆ Glycogen stores begin to empty. Your body has burned through most of the glucose from your last meal and starts tapping liver glycogen.
◆ Insulin drops significantly, signaling your body to switch fuel sources. Growth hormone begins a gradual rise.
◆ Early fat mobilization begins — free fatty acids start entering the bloodstream.
24 Hours
Ketosis Onset
◆ Ketosis begins. Liver glycogen is largely depleted and your liver ramps up ketone body production (BHB, acetoacetate) from fatty acids.
◆ Autophagy activates. With nutrient signaling quiet, your cells begin recycling damaged proteins and organelles for energy.
◆ Growth hormone surges — up to 2–3× baseline — to protect lean muscle during the fuel switch.
48 Hours
Deep Autophagy
◆ Autophagy intensifies significantly. Your cells aggressively clear misfolded proteins, damaged mitochondria, and intracellular debris.
◆ Growth hormone peaks — can reach up to 5× baseline levels, strongly preserving muscle mass while fat burning accelerates.
◆ Inflammation markers (CRP, IL-6) begin to drop. Many people report increased mental clarity as the brain adapts to ketone fuel.
72 Hours
Immune Reset
◆ Immune system regeneration begins. Old and damaged white blood cells are broken down and recycled. Stem cell-based regeneration of new immune cells is triggered.
◆ IGF-1 drops substantially, which is linked to reduced cancer risk, slower aging pathways, and enhanced cellular repair.
◆ Ketone levels are fully elevated. Your body is now efficiently running on fat as its primary fuel source.
Day 4
Deep Ketosis & Repair
◆ Fat adaptation is well established. Hunger often fades significantly as your brain fully transitions to ketone metabolism.
◆ BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) increases, supporting neural repair, synaptic plasticity, and cognitive function.
◆ Gut lining begins a rest-and-repair cycle. Intestinal stem cells become more active without the demands of constant digestion.
Day 5
Cellular Renewal
◆ Peak autophagy window. The body's cellular cleanup and recycling machinery is operating at maximum capacity.
◆ Insulin sensitivity is dramatically improved. Cells become highly responsive to even trace amounts of insulin.
◆ Senescent ("zombie") cells — old cells that refuse to die and cause inflammation — become more vulnerable to clearance.
Day 6
Stem Cell Activation
◆ Stem cell production increases. Your body begins priming new cells to replace what autophagy has cleared, setting the stage for regeneration upon refeeding.
◆ Inflammatory markers reach their lowest point. Systemic inflammation is significantly reduced across the body.
◆ Electrolyte management becomes critical — sodium, potassium, and magnesium need close attention at this stage.
Day 7
Full Regeneration Mode
◆ The body is primed for total renewal. When you refeed, stem cells flood the system to rebuild the immune system, gut lining, and other tissues from the ground up.
◆ Gene expression has shifted. Longevity-associated genes (sirtuins, FOXO) are upregulated. Pro-aging and pro-inflammatory pathways are suppressed.
◆ Refeeding strategy matters enormously at this point — breaking the fast with bone broth or gentle foods protects the gut and maximizes the regenerative window.
Extended fasting should be undertaken with medical guidance.
Electrolytes, hydration, and careful refeeding are essential for safety.
